Miniature Robotic Arm

Overview

This table-top miniature robotic arm was designed in Fusion 360 to be completely 3D-printable. It features 4 degrees of freedom, each powered by an SG90 servo motor, and all controlled by an ESP-32 microcontroller. The arm also requires no extra hardware or fasteners outside of what is supplied with the servo motors by embedding the servo horns into the arm links before securing them to the motor.

The robot is powered by a USB-C connection and can be controlled through that connection serially, or controlled wirelessly through the ESP-32's Wi-Fi Access Point. Inverse kinematics calculations allow for x, y, z coordinates to be translated to servo angles. This robot, and the experience gained with servos and microcontrollers, served as the basis for some of my later robotics projects, such as the Remote Control Robot Vacuum. In the future, I would like to build a larger version with larger motors and gearboxes, capable of PID control and trajectory planning.
