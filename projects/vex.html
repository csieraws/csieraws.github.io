Robotics & Controls

VEX Robotics Competition

Overview

VEX is a middle-school and high-school level robotics competition with a unique yearly challenge. I participated in VEX from 7th grade until graduating high-school, which gave me significant experience in both mechanical design and programming. Throughout my participation in high-school, my team reached the State Championship each year, and reached the World Championship twice.

I gained experience in Fusion 360 by modelling several robots each year, and by building robots to complete various tasks, I constructed mechanical systems such as 4-bar linkages, differentials, ratchets, and flywheels. I also developed autonomous routines through wheel odometry and PID control, done in C++. This allowed for precise point-to-point movements with high repeatability. This involved integrating several sensors, such as IMUs, encoders, potentiometers, and ultrasonic sensors.
